Lesson · Controlling the cook with a VLM

Teaching a vision-language model to cook.

A streaming VLM watches the game and gives orders in plain language — "chop the potato", "ladle the pot cooking rice onto my plate", "serve". A small converter turns each sentence into the exact button presses the game needs. This page shows how — try the live grounding demo below.

The idea

Two levels of control

The VLM shouldn't have to micro-manage every footstep. It speaks in skills; the converter handles the footwork. (It can still drop to raw buttons when it wants fine control — just slower.)

⌨️ Low level — what the engine runs

6 buttons, one per tick. Every interaction is the same button.

▲ north▼ south◀ west▶ east● interact… stay

To chop a potato: walk, walk, turn, interact, interact, interact, interact, interact… dozens of presses.

🗣️ High level — what the VLM says

Natural-language skills. One sentence = a whole sub-task.

"chop the potato"
"ladle the pot cooking rice onto my plate"
"serve"

Each compiles to the right button sequence automatically.

It's closed-loop, not a fixed recipe of presses. Each tick the converter looks at the live game and decides the next button — so it keeps pressing interact until the potato is actually chopped, walks around a cook who's in the way, and faces the right tile first. The VLM says what; the loop figures out how.
The vocabulary

The skills (verbs)

Every skill compiles to the same primitive: walk next to the target → turn to face it → press interact as many times as needed. The verb just decides the target and the stop condition.

The hard part

Grounding: which "pot" do you mean?

Plain language is ambiguous — say "the pot" when two pots are on the stove and the converter can't act. The fix: let the VLM attach a qualifier that pins down the exact object. These inject spatial & relational meaning into the sentence:

QualifierSay it likeResolves by
content"the pot cooking rice"what's inside / its job
state"the done pot", "the empty board", "the dirty plate"the station's status
spatial — absolute"the left pot", "the top-left pan"position on the grid
spatial — relative"the nearest pot"distance to the cook
identity"pot 2"stations numbered top-to-bottom
exact"the pot at (2,0)"a precise tile (escape hatch)
💡 Tip for grounding from video: overlay small ID badges (and a faint coordinate grid) on each station in the stream the VLM watches. Then it can always fall back to "pot 2" or "(2,0)" when words alone are ambiguous — the picture supplies the names.
